I think to appreciate the TV Typewriter you have to appreciate how freaking expensive printers and typewriters were relative to the cost of the computer[1]. Don't TVT was 16 x 32 because that was exactly 1024 characters which fit in one static RAM chip. The Apple ][ could, and did display 40 columns (also 16 rows) on an unmodified TV. That kind of output was SO MUCH more useful than looking at the blinking lights on the front panel. It was also cheaper than what a 24 line by 80 character terminal would cost you.

As the Hackaday piece says, the TV Typewriter was given to the world by the recently departed Don Lancaster:

https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=36545595

My dad built one of Lancaster's TV Typewriters back in the '70s for a black & white TV studio system. It was a huge improvement in speed and correctibility for static onscreen text overlayed on the video output, when previously it was done with physical hardware and chroma key using white letters on a black background.

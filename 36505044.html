While better than the US, keeping "better" insulin from poor people means that poor people die more.

"Better" insulin generally actually works better, is easier to get the right dose, and is often longer-acting and more effective. Cheap insulin - often older formulations - make it far easier to accidentally kill yourself.

I'll also note that the generic, older insulin products are fairly inexpensive in the US, though folks still find themselves tempted to ration. And you just have to risk death more with them. A few other vital medicines have cheap generics as well.

But not all vital medicines have generics, and some generics are still expensive. Some of those vital medicines keep people alive.

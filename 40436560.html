My current workplace is pretty lean and flexible when it comes to this kind of thing, and we've tried a few different project management solutions over the years. Without fail, the new solution looks great at first glance and feels great as we start using it. Then it gradually becomes annoying and horrible just like the last one, and people start yearning for Jira. The horrible-ness usually comes from straight up bugs, a lack of comprehensive reporting, weak integrations with other tools, and/or a UX that doesn't jive with certain roles/people.

I think it'd be a real challenge to meaningfully compete with Jira. You'd need to not only match its feature set but also be faster than it and less buggy. It seems that being a "not Jira" gets you a good amount of customers off the bat due to how much people loath Jira, but it'll probably take a paradigm shift or a very competent team to overthrow it.

Building one’s business solely dependent on the grace of another’s API access is a pretty precarious position. Beyond that the incumbent APIs are usually slow and severely rate limited. Building improvements on top would necessitate a complex sync/caching system, plus if you want to layer on real time collaboration your job is even harder since you need to maintain your own state (for collab in your CRDT format or whatever) that you periodically reconcile with the underlying tool. At that point, you already need a complete backend so you might as well be a stand alone product and just offer 2-way sync with the incumbent as a value add feature.

Codepen plays nice and just uses this for logging purposes. But when other sites take more aggressive anti-ad-block measures your ad-blocker can still defeat them by silently redirecting some analytics/ad-serving JavaScipt to an internal no-op resource. That way the error handler isn't called but the JS doesn't actually run. Or the JS is replaced with a no-op equivalent. See uBlock's resource library here: https://github.com/gorhill/uBlock/wiki/Resources-Library

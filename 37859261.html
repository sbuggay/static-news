You can make stereograms with your Lytro, but you're limited to center to center distances less than the diameter of the lens.

I was surprised when the Lytro came out and it was so tiny. To be properly useful, it needed about a 10" diameter outer lens.

There are ways to take multiple conventional exposures from the same plane, and generate a virtual focal plane after the fact. I've been manually doing that (using Hugin and a python script) since I heard (in 2008) of Marc Levoy's experiments at Stanford.

That's not Stable Diffusion though, it's a different third-party model. (MiDaS, for example, but there are many of those)

Generally, good depth maps is a holy grail of imaging, but the issue is that it's very sensitive to resolution and correctness, so it must be perfect and have better resolution than the picture to achieve good results. ML-powered depth estimation isn't there yet.

Holographic images still blow my mind. There's something amazing about a 3D scene, complete with parallax and depth of field, being encoded on a 2D plane.

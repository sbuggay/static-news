There is actually a third point to this whole idea. Given that most Lemmy admins were not interested in being a home to the "mirrored" communities, I'm building a set of different Lemmy instances that will be focused on specific niches or interests, such as:

    - https://soccer.forum, https://nba.space, https://nfl.community, https://matchpoint.zone  (sport news, fan groups, discussion, etc)
    - https://metacritics.zone, https://roll20.top (pop-culture, movies, TV shows, games)
    - blockchained.world (web3)
    - https://selfhosted.forum (devops, homelab)
    - https://style.land (brands, fashion, consumer items)
    - https://hi-fi.community (audio), https://viewfinder.pro (photography)
    - https://hardware.watch (tech reviews and news, pc enthusiasts, etc)
    - https://gearhead.town (cars)
    - https://netheads.online (voip)
These lemmy instances are meant to be only destination hubs. They are not open for registration, but I am planning to let people decide what communities should be opened on those instances. I've created those because it seems that a lot of people are signing up to an instance and then fail at discovering the content.

grues-dinner|parent|next|

A basic consideration of the energy involved should tell you.

355mm radius disc, mass 1kg, moment of inertia is 1/4 mr², so 0.03 kg.m². This ignores all inertia of the arbor and motor rotor, mind, which is possibly significant (smaller radius but dense), Say it spins at 4000rpm, or 420 rad/s (some saws go to 6000 plus and this is quadratic).

So kinetic energy in the blade alone is 1/2Iw², or 0.5 * 0.03 * 420², or 2.6kJ. For comparison, a rifle bullet is about that, which seems right on the face of it.

So to remove that energy in 5ms (SawStop's claim) is 520kW, or a current of 2000A at 240V, or 4000A at 120V.

This is not completely technically impossible to deliver - you need about 200 0.1 farad capacitors charged to 250V, which are 100mm is diameter and 250mm tall each, so a fridge-sized box. Actually pushing 2000A into a motor winding for 5ms isn't actually that easy as it's a canonical example of an inductive load, so you need even more, plus hopefully a way to stop the current when done before turning the inside of the motor into a plasma ball. Evaporating the motor winding before you've stopped the blade is no good, so you'll need to uprate the coils massively, which will make the motor very heavy and very expensive.

So, you won't break physics to do this, but it will be large, heavy and expensive.

davesmylie|root|parent|next|

You can use wet wood or pressure treated lumber or even foil coated acrylic - but the key is that you need to be expecting this, and you put the saw in to by-pass mode.

At that point, it's just another dumb saw that will chop your finger off, but it won't trigger the cartridge, and you can make what ever cuts you need.

The way it tries to determine if it's wet wood / a body part is the capacitance change. Slightly different profile which they can use to make an educated guess (obviously erring on the side of caution).

This is why for some time they would give you a free cartridge if yours triggered on flesh - they wanted the data on there from real-life flesh contact to improve their calculations.

It's probably not a popular perspective,1 but to me these are still "just" websites. That's what they are, after all. Under the rules of the www game, anyone can have a website. A billion people could have websites each having less than 100 pages with content created by the website operator, a single website could have a billion pages of content created by other people, and so on. Facebook, Threads, etc. is still one person's website. Sure, there is a corporation behind it; the domain name, the servers, the PHP, etc., the assets required to run a website, are owned by the company, but as the company is set up, one person has _total_ control over it.

1. The problem I see is that some folks think the www only exists as a venue for having popularity contests. And all use of it is 100% commercial. That's wrong IMO. It was never intended to be that way and it does not have to be that way.

"After birth, heart cells go through changes where they switch from using sugar to fat for energy and they stop dividing, which makes it hard for an adult heart to heal after injury. This study tried to change this by stopping heart cells from using fat for energy. They found that doing this made heart cells more resistant to low oxygen levels and let them divide again, helping the heart heal after injury. They discovered that changes in energy use caused an increase of a specific substance (alpha-ketoglutarate) in heart cells. This activated a specific enzyme, KDM5, which altered genes that were making heart cells mature and therefore less able to divide. They concluded that changing how heart cells use energy can affect their ability to divide and heal the heart."

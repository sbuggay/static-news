At the time, in my circle, the Adaptec cards had high geek cred. Adaptec meant you were graduating to SCSI. SCSI meant you could use cool disk drives, and tape drives, and CD-ROM drives, and plotters, and .. and .. it’s SCSI! The world is my oyster! The USB of the early 90s.

Sure everything was more expensive, sure you probably never used it to it’s potential. But SCSI was good Gear. Geeks dig gear, the potential with Gear, the endless possibilities with Gear. And, of course, the posing and preening that goes along with Gear.

“Oh you got a tape backup? Is it . . . SCSI? You know with SCSI the drives can stream simultaneously with less bus contention via DMA while the drive is seeking, improving throughput and utilization on the channels with the daisy chained devices. You know that’s cool, right?”

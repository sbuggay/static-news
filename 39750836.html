> Point of view is worth 80 IQ points —ACK

I currently like the https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Knowledge_space antimatroid model; think of it as a graph, or like tech/skill-trees in video games. Sometimes you can unlock a new skill simply by combining (taking the join of) two skills you already have mastered (X ∨ Y = Z); other times the only way to get to a new skill is to make some sort of 'leap' from a single existing skill; these are much more difficult because (Y ∨ Y = Y) so you need inspiration to get from Y to Z.

This is one of the most powerful things you can learn to do. Question your own assumptions, with the goal of seeing what is holding you back. It literally unblocks you and allows you to do things you never considered doing before.

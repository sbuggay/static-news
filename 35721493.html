gcr|root|parent|next|

It's not meaningless, it's a significant next step for AWU. As a strategy, AWU's been focusing on unionizing Alphabet TVCs at several small shops for years now. I personally think this is partly because small TVC groups are easier to organize than PAs, and partly because TVCs are so badly treated compared to full-timers that they're more likely to view unions favorably. (It's hard as hell to organize FTEs for various reasons.)

However, as I understand it, a key problem is that Google wasn't required to deal with AWU directly before now, because they've been able to say that Cognizant and other vendors are the employers. That said, the status quo was still a win for workers because AWU could bargain with vendors, but it's not as impactful as it would be if Google were required to bargain with AWU directly.

That's apparently changed. If the NLRB rules that Google and the contractor are co-employers, then that could require Google to bargain directly with this unit.

As for what is bargained, I understand that companies are typically required to work with the union "in good faith" to come to an agreement about hours, wages, policies, layoffs, etc, and there are typically consequences (strikes, fines, etc) if they don't. At least there used to be -- labor laws are significantly weaker these past few decades...

    Let's imagine a library full of books to explain PostgreSQL's isolation levels:
Read Uncommitted (Lowest Security): In this library, you can take a peek at a book even if someone is in the middle of writing in it. It's like reading someone's story while they're still typing it. This can be chaotic and confusing.

Read Committed (Medium Security): In this library, you can only read a book once someone has finished writing a whole chapter. You don't see unfinished sentences, so it's a bit more organized, but things can still change while you're reading.

Repeatable Read (Higher Security): In this library, when you start reading a book, it's frozen. Even if someone adds or changes a chapter in the book, you won't see those changes until you finish reading and start a new book. It's like your book is in a protective bubble while you're reading.

Serializable (Highest Security): In this library, you get the whole bookshelf to yourself. Nobody can touch the books you're reading until you're done. It's like having your own personal copy of a book, and no one else can access it until you're finished.

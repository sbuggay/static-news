The poem discussed has been translated many times in many different ways. There’s even a book, by Hiroaki Sato, that collects more than a hundred versions in English [1]. Here are a few:

R. H. Blyth

The old pond.

A frog jumps in—

Plop!

Clara A. Walsh

An old-time pond, from off whose shadowed depth

Is heard the splash where some lithe frog leaps in.

Inazo Nitobe

Into a old pond

A frog took a sudden plunge,

Then is heard a splash.

Harold G. Henderson

An ancient pond;

Plash of the water

When a frog jumps in.

Allen Ginsberg

The old pond

A frog jumped in,

Kerplunk!

Limerick

There once was a curious frog

Who sat by a pond on a log

And, to see what resulted,

In the pond catapulted

With a water-noise heard round the bog.

[1] https://search.worldcat.org/title/31783352

What is the upper bound to human productivity? Can we define it?

A simplistic counter argument might suggest that given enough technological advancement, humanity would expand beyond the finite limits of the Earth. However, it is also probable that technologies which are inconceivable to us today will become available in the future. How this will change our understanding of the Earth's perceived "ecological limits" is unknown.

As we project out into the future, we are dealing with things which are known to be unknown-unknowns.

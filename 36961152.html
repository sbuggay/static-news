The Sydney Harbor Tunnel had this problem. After an over-height truck got wedged in the tunnel and took three weeks to remove, the tunnel operators had to do something drastic. They already had multiple sets of detectors, flashing lights, signs, and movable bars hanging down to bang on overheight trucks. That wasn't enough.

So they had something custom built, which is now a product called Softstop.[1] When the detectors sense an over-height truck, water jets shoot downward across the road. Then, a very high powered projector projects a giant STOP sign on the water curtain, right in front of the truck. That seems to successfully get truckers attention.

Softstop is a complex installation, with pumps, tanks, drains, lights, and controls. It's probably overkill for some minor underpass.

Other countries use a much cheaper method.

At some distance before the tunnel or passage under a bridge with limited height (but after the signs that advertise this) there is a solid metal barrier at the height limit and when any too high truck attempts to go forward (which of course happens from time to time) the barrier cuts the top of the truck, which is guaranteed to attract the attention of the drivers (and presumably the anger of their employer).

EDIT: I have posted this before watching the video, which shows exactly how this method, of using a steel "crash beam" works.

In this case, the truck was only slightly above the height limit, but in another place I have seen a similar incident where the truck was higher so all its roof has been cleanly cut and detached from the truck.

This is the right method of handling such height limits, the only one that is guaranteed to be foolproof.

Even if it damages the vehicle, if the vehicle were able to pass with an extra height, it is certain that it would be damaged anyway, but in worse conditions, especially in the case of a tunnel.

Other countries do not use this excuse, which actually is irrational.

If a resistant barrier is put before the tunnel at the height limit, it will damage any too high vehicle that attempts to pass, but removing the barrier guarantees that the vehicle will be damaged later, in worse conditions, inside the tunnel.

If the vehicle stops later inside the tunnel, instead of being blocked before it in some place with good visibility, then the road conditions will certainly be even unsafer.
